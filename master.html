Identifying Dangerous Goods

Purolator is a leader in the transport of dangerous goods (DG) in Canada and accepts shipments for both air and ground transport. Shipments containing dangerous goods must be tendered to Purolator in accordance with the Transportation of Dangerous Goods (TDG) Act and regulations for ground shipments and the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO) Technical Instructions for air shipments. Purolator’s terms and conditions contain further information.



Dangerous goods are articles or substances that are capable of posing a risk to health, safety, property or the environment and are subject to government regulations. Dangerous goods are not always obvious and many items are, or contain, regulated substances. Some common items are considered dangerous goods such as: some paint, aero-sols, lithium batteries (either separate or contained in cell phones, laptops, video cameras, etc.), dry ice, perfumes,



airbags, ammunition, butane, reworks, gasoline, lighters, car batteries, matches, nail polish, nail polish remover, solvents, some chemicals, and more. If you are unsure whether your item is regulated by TDG or ICAO, please check

Compliance Requirements

  1. 500 kg exemption
    1. Only those dangerous goods listed in section 1.16 of the TDG Regulations are acceptable for the 500 kg exemption.
    2. Each piece of the shipment must weigh 30 kg or less and be marked and labeled according to the TDG Regulations.
    3. The shipper is not required to provide dangerous goods documentation.
    4. 500 kg applies to the total amount on any one vehicle.
    5. Acceptable for transport by road vehicle only.
    6. This exemption is not allowed for Purolator Freight shipments.
    7. Purolator’s Dangerous Goods surcharge is applicable.
    8. For more details, please refer to the 500 kg Exemption job aid.
  2. Limited Quantities (Ground Only)
    1. Replaces the former Consumer Commodity designation
    2. Refers to shipments restricted in quantity and offered for transport without documentation or hazard labels.
    3. Must be marked with one of the acceptable Limited Quantity markings.

    5. Purolator’s Dangerous Goods surcharge will not apply, but Special Handling may apply as these shipments require manual handling.
